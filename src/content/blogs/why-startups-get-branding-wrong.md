Most startups treat branding as an afterthought. They build the product first, slap a logo on it, pick a random color from a palette generator, and call it done.

Then they wonder why nobody remembers them.

## Branding Is Not Design

Design is what your brand looks like. Branding is what your brand **feels** like. It's the story you tell, the promise you make, and the emotion you create every time someone interacts with you.

## The 3 Layers of Brand Identity

**1. Core Identity** — Who are you? What do you stand for? What's your mission beyond making money?

**2. Visual Identity** — Logo, colors, typography, photography style. This is what most people think branding is. It's only 30% of it.

**3. Voice & Tone** — How do you speak? Are you bold and edgy, or calm and trustworthy? Consistency here builds recognition faster than any visual.

## The Most Common Mistakes

- Copying competitors instead of carving a unique position
- Changing brand direction every 6 months
- Not defining the target audience before designing anything
- Treating branding as a one-time project instead of an ongoing investment

## The Fix

Before you hire a designer, answer these questions:
1. Who is your ideal customer?
2. What emotion do you want them to feel?
3. What's one word you want associated with your brand?

Build everything else around those answers.
